CATEGORY: K-Lifestyle
STYLE REFERENCE: Kinfolk, Well+Good, Monocle
READER GOAL: I want to understand this way of living — and bring something home

WRITING STYLE:
- Open with a scene. Specific. Present tense. No preamble.
  Not "Korea has a concept called..." but a moment where the concept lives.
- Define Korean concepts through behaviour, not dictionary entries
- Write slowly. This category rewards patience. One good paragraph
  beats three rushed ones.
- Find the universal underneath the specific Korean detail
- End with something the reader can actually do tomorrow morning

SCENE-FIRST PHILOSOPHY — BAD vs GOOD:
  Bad:  "Nunchi is a Korean concept about reading the room and being
         socially aware of others around you."
  Good: "She noticed his cup was empty before he did. She filled it
         without being asked, without breaking the conversation.
         This is nunchi — the Korean art of attention so refined
         that care becomes invisible."

  Bad:  "Koreans have a strong culture of rest and self-care."
  Good: "After dinner, nothing. No plans, no productivity, no phones
         face-up on the table. Just the particular satisfaction of
         doing exactly what you feel like, which tonight is nothing.
         Koreans call this time 'siwon hada' — the cool relief of
         an obligation-free hour."

  Bad:  "The Korean concept of jeong refers to a deep emotional bond."
  Good: "You don't fall into jeong. It accumulates — in shared meals,
         in small favours given and received, in the slow weight of
         years. By the time you notice it, it's already holding you."

ARTICLE STRUCTURE:
1. The scene — a specific, present-tense moment where the concept lives.
   Three to five sentences. No Korean vocabulary yet.
2. Name and define the concept — in plain English first, Korean term second
3. The root — where this idea comes from (Confucian, Buddhist, historical, practical)
4. How it lives today — modern Koreans, changing interpretations, tensions
5. The takeaway — one concrete thing the reader can bring into their own day

TONE: Unhurried. Attentive. Like reading a very good Sunday magazine profile.
The writer notices things most people walk past.

HEADLINE STYLE:
  "The Korean [Concept] That [What It Gives You]"
  "Why Koreans [Behaviour] — And What Happens When You Try It"
  "The [One Word] Philosophy Behind Korea's [Outcome]"
  e.g. "The Korean Idea of Rest That Has Nothing to Do With Sleep"
  e.g. "Why Koreans Fill Your Cup Before You Ask"
