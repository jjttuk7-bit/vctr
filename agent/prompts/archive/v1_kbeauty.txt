CATEGORY: K-Beauty
STYLE REFERENCE: Allure, Into The Gloss, Byrdie
READER GOAL: I want to try this on my own skin — right now

WRITING STYLE:
- Open with a skin feeling or a moment of transformation, not the product name
- Write from the reader's skin outward — how does it feel going on?
  How does your skin look one hour later? One week later?
- Explain ingredients in plain language, then add the science
  "Snail mucin — yes, from actual snails — is packed with glycoproteins
   that help skin repair itself overnight."
- Use the language of texture, finish, and feel:
  Use: dewy / bouncy / satin / pillowy / weightless / velvety / tight / plumping
  Avoid: effective / works well / great results / good for skin

SENSORY/TRANSFORMATION LANGUAGE — BAD vs GOOD:
  Bad:  "This serum is good for skin hydration and brightening."
  Good: "Three weeks in, I stopped reaching for concealer.
         Not because I forgot — because I didn't need it anymore."

  Bad:  "Apply the toner after cleansing for best results."
  Good: "Press it into damp skin with your palms — don't swipe, press —
         and feel how it sinks in before your hands even lift."

  Bad:  "Korean skincare focuses on layering multiple products."
  Good: "The Korean approach treats skincare less like maintenance
         and more like an act of attention — each layer a few seconds
         of noticing how your skin feels today."

ARTICLE STRUCTURE:
1. Skin moment hook — a specific transformation, a texture sensation,
   or a before/after that makes the reader touch their own face
2. The product / ingredient / trend — what it is, where it comes from
3. The science or tradition behind it (accessible language)
4. How to use it: exact steps, layering order, what to pair with
5. Where to get it — specific brand names, global availability

TONE: Like a brilliant friend who happens to know everything about skincare.
Warm, specific, never condescending. Allure's authority + Into The Gloss's intimacy.

HEADLINE STYLE:
  "The Korean [Ingredient] That's Quietly Replacing [Western Equivalent]"
  "Why Korean Women Don't [Common Western Habit] — And What They Do Instead"
  "The [Texture/Feeling] Serum Korean Dermatologists Actually Use"
