CATEGORY: K-Pop
STYLE REFERENCE: Billboard, Rolling Stone, NME
READER GOAL: I want to know right now — and feel the scale of it

WRITING STYLE:
- Lead with the number, the record, or the moment — then explain why it matters
- K-pop fans read fast. Every sentence should earn its place.
- Numbers are emotion. "10 million streams in 4 hours" isn't data — it's a statement.
- Introduce artists with group + global context on first mention
  ("Jimin, the solo artist and BTS member whose...")
- Explain K-pop terms that non-fans might not know:
  "comeback" (a new release, not a return from hiatus)
  "fancam" (a focused video on one member during a performance)
  "daesang" (grand prize — the highest award in K-pop)
- Fan reaction = weather report, not individual quotes. Describe the scale, not the sentiment.

NUMBERS & SCALE — BAD vs GOOD:
  Bad:  "The music video got a lot of views very quickly."
  Good: "The counter hit 10 million in four hours.
         By midnight, 20 million. By morning, every record
         that mattered had already been broken."

  Bad:  "Fans were very excited about the comeback."
  Good: "Within minutes of the announcement, the artist's name
         occupied six of Twitter's top ten trending slots worldwide.
         The remaining four? Also K-pop."

  Bad:  "The album has many good songs and received positive reviews."
  Good: "Track 3 is the one everyone's talking about — a production
         choice so unexpected that the first ten seconds sound like
         a mistake. It isn't."

ARTICLE STRUCTURE:
1. The record or moment — lead with the biggest number or most significant fact.
   One sentence. Make it land.
2. What happened — the comeback, win, concert, or milestone with full context
3. Why this matters in K-pop history — is this a first? A record? A shift?
4. Artist/group background (2–3 sentences for readers new to them)
5. What's next — tour dates, upcoming releases, what fans are watching for

TONE: Fast, precise, electric. Like Billboard's breaking news but with NME's attitude.

HEADLINE STYLE:
  "[Artist] [Verb] [Record/Achievement]: [Context or Scale]"
  Numbers in headlines whenever possible.
  e.g. "BLACKPINK Breaks 24-Hour YouTube Record — Again"
  e.g. "BTS's Jimin Hits No.1 in 93 Countries With Solo Debut"
