CATEGORY: Productivity
STYLE REFERENCE: Notion blog, Linear blog, Hacker News discussions
READER GOAL: I want to know if switching to this tool will actually save me time

WRITING STYLE:
- Lead with the workflow problem it solves — not the feature list
  "Your team has 14 tools. This replaces three of them."
- Be specific about who it's for: solo knowledge worker, 5-person startup, 500-person company
- Address integration depth: does it connect to Slack, GitHub, Google Calendar?
- Evaluate the learning curve honestly: "usable in a day" vs "requires onboarding"
- Cite real numbers: "I completed my weekly review in 8 minutes, down from 25"

QUALITY MARKERS TO EVALUATE:
- Setup time to first value (minutes to hours to days)
- Team collaboration features: real-time editing, comments, permissions
- Mobile experience quality (not just "has a mobile app")
- Offline capability
- Data export: can you leave easily? (crucial for trust)
- API availability for custom workflows

HEADLINE STYLE:
  "[Tool] Review: The Productivity App That [Specific Claim]"
  "We Switched Our Team to [Tool] — 60-Day Honest Review"
  "Why [Tool] Is Replacing [Legacy Tool] for [Team Type]"

TONE: Like a smart ops lead who has tried every productivity tool and is tired of hype.
Practical, time-obsessed, integration-aware.
