CATEGORY: K-Drama
STYLE REFERENCE: Entertainment Weekly, Vulture, The Atlantic
READER GOAL: I want to decide whether to watch this — and feel something first

WRITING STYLE:
- Drop the reader into the emotional world BEFORE explaining the plot
- Write about tension, atmosphere, and stakes — not just what happens
- NEVER spoil key plot twists without a warning
- Introduce actors with their most recognizable international work
  ("Song Hye-kyo, known internationally for Descendants of the Sun...")
- Explain Korean TV landscape briefly when relevant
  ("tvN is South Korea's premium cable network, home to hits like Crash Landing on You")
- Let the cultural context breathe — weave it in, don't footnote it

TONE WORDS TO USE:
  gripping / slow-burn / gut-punch / achingly / inevitable / restrained /
  devastating / quietly / electric / unravels / haunting
AVOID:
  amazing / incredible / must-watch / best ever / perfect / awesome

SENSORY/EMOTIONAL LANGUAGE — BAD vs GOOD:
  Bad:  "The drama is about a romance between two people from different worlds."
  Good: "By episode three, you're not watching a love story anymore —
         you're watching two people negotiate the distance between
         who they are and who they're allowed to be."

  Bad:  "The acting is really good and the story is interesting."
  Good: "Lee Jung-jae doesn't need dialogue. A single held breath,
         a door closed too carefully — the restraint is what breaks you."

  Bad:  "This is a new Netflix drama that just premiered."
  Good: "Netflix dropped all eight episodes on a Friday. By Sunday,
         it was trending in 34 countries. Nobody who started it stopped."

ARTICLE STRUCTURE:
1. Emotional hook — a scene, a feeling, a question the show raises.
   Make the reader feel something before they know what they're reading about.
2. What it is — title, genre, platform, episode count, premiere date
3. Why non-Korean viewers should care — the universal human theme
4. Cast and creator context (brief, only what matters)
5. Where to watch internationally + cultural note if needed

HEADLINE STYLE:
  "[Show Name]: [Emotional Claim]"
  e.g. "The Drama That Made 34 Countries Forget to Sleep"
  e.g. "Why [Show] Hits Different If You've Ever Felt Invisible"
