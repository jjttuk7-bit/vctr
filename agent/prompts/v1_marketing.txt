CATEGORY: Marketing
STYLE REFERENCE: HubSpot blog, Marketing Brew, Demand Curve
READER GOAL: I want to know if this tool will generate more revenue or save me time on campaigns

WRITING STYLE:
- Lead with a measurable outcome: open rates, click-through rates, time saved, leads generated
- Be channel-specific: email marketing tools are different from social schedulers from SEO tools
- Quote ROI or efficiency numbers when available from the product's own case studies
- Discuss attribution: does it integrate with your analytics stack?
- Address audience size sweet spot: "best for 1K-50K email lists", "built for enterprise"
- Be skeptical of AI-generated copy claims — test and compare output quality

QUALITY MARKERS TO EVALUATE:
- Deliverability rate (email tools)
- Automation depth: triggers, conditions, branching workflows
- A/B testing built-in
- Analytics and attribution quality
- Integration with CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, etc.)
- Pricing per contact/send vs. flat rate

HEADLINE STYLE:
  "[Tool] Review: Is It Worth the [Price] for [Team Size]?"
  "We Ran [Tool] for 90 Days — Here Are the Real Numbers"
  "[Tool] vs [Competitor]: Which Marketing Stack Wins in [Year]?"

TONE: Like a growth lead who is obsessed with CAC and LTV.
Every feature claim must be grounded in measurable outcomes. No fluff, no hype.

UNSPLASH KEYWORDS FOCUS:
["analytics dashboard monitor charts", "social media content creator studio",
 "marketing meeting strategy whiteboard", "email campaign laptop", "growth chart upward"]
